The Suite

What Is Oracle EPM Cloud?

Oracle EPM Cloud — Enterprise Performance Management — is a suite of cloud applications that covers the complete financial intelligence lifecycle of a large organisation. It is not one product. It is six interconnected modules, each solving a different part of the same problem: how does an organisation of 5,000+ people, across multiple countries and currencies, produce reliable financial information in time to act on it?

The suite spans three domains. Planning — what should happen (budgets, forecasts, workforce plans, capital plans). Close & Consolidation — what did happen (statutory consolidation, account reconciliation, currency translation, intercompany elimination). Governance — the master data and integration layer that ensures the numbers moving between systems are the same numbers (Enterprise Data Management, Data Integration).

Most organisations implement these modules sequentially over several years. A typical journey: EPBCS first for planning, FCCS next for statutory close, EDMCS to govern the chart of accounts, ARCS to automate reconciliations, with Integration connecting to SAP or Oracle Financials throughout. Understanding how they connect is what separates an EPM practitioner from an EPM architect.

Step 01
Master Data
EDMCS defines the chart of accounts, entity hierarchy, and consolidation rules that every other module uses. Change the chart of accounts in EDMCS — it propagates to FCCS, EPBCS, and ARCS automatically.
EDMCS
Step 02
Planning & Forecast
EPBCS builds the budget and rolling forecast. Finance enters headcount plans, revenue drivers, and OpEx assumptions. The approved Budget becomes a scenario in FCCS — the target the close process measures against.
EPBCS
Step 03
Trial Balance Load
Integration pulls trial balances from SAP, Oracle Financials, and other ERPs. Data lands in FCCS as Entity Input. ARCS reconciles sub-ledger accounts to GL before the load — certifying that what enters FCCS is clean.
Integration ARCS
Step 04
Consolidation & Close
FCCS translates currencies, eliminates intercompany transactions, applies ownership percentages, and runs the consolidation sequence. The CFO sees one set of group numbers — across 50 entities, 8 currencies, in 4 hours.
FCCS
Step 05
Reporting & Disclosure
Narrative Reporting wraps consolidated FCCS results in management commentary, IFRS disclosures, and board packs. The CFO approves. The period locks. Reports burst to subsidiary controllers. The close is complete.
Narrative Reporting
